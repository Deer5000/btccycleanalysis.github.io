What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that enables low-cost payments without the need for central authorities and issuers. Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer (P2P) currency system created in open source C++ programming code. Bitcoins can be accessed from anywhere in the world with an internet connection. Once a user has Bitcoins, they are stored in a digital wallet. Bitcoins can then be sent to anyone else who has a Bitcoin address. Bitcoin was developed in 2009 and based on the works of an individual or group of individuals known as Satoshi Nakamoto.

What is a cycle?

A cycle can be considered as a "halving event", where the supply of bitcoin cuts in half. This happens once every four years, and is believed to be in the middle of the market cycle. This is what happened in 2012, 2016, and recently 2020.

Cycle 1 (2012)

- Halved on November 1st 2012

- Supply cut from 168M BTC to 84M BTC

Cycle 2 (2016)

- Halved on July 1st 2016

- Supply cut to 42M BTC

Cycle 3 (2020)

- Halved on May 1st 2020

- Supply cut to 21M BTC
